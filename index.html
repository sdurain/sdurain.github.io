Selected thesis projects

Uncertainty and sensitivity analysis with deep ensembles

Uncertainty Surrogate model Sensitivity analysis
  • Goal: Quantify feature importance and prediction uncertainty for braking stability using limited simulation data.
  • Data generation: Small physics-based simulation dataset from complex eigenvalue analysis (CEA).
  • Approach: Deep-ensemble surrogate model based on 10 MLPs.
  • Result: Sensitivity analysis accounting for epistemic uncertainty.
  • Demo: Beam notebook ↗

RNN surrogate modeling for nonlinear braking dynamics

Surrogate model Nonlinear dynamics Long-time forecasting
  • Goal: Develop a surrogate model of nonlinear system dynamics for long-time forecasting.
  • Data generation: High-fidelity reduced-order simulations based on the Craig–Bampton method.
  • Approach: RNN-based surrogate model trained on spectrogram representations.
  • Result: High-fidelity predictions with demonstrated extrapolation capabilities.
  • Demo: Duffing oscillator notebook ↗

Brake pad semantic segmentation

Semantic segmentation Transfer learning Transformer
  • Goal: Quantify and track tribological surface evolution at the brake contact interface.
  • Data generation: Multimodal dataset of optical and SEM images with pseudo-annotations.
  • Approach: Fine-tuned SegFormer model using transfer learning.
  • Result: Accurate semantic masks enabling quantitative monitoring of material classes and surface evolution.

Experimental and numerical data acquisition for brake squeal

Multimodal acquisition Signal processing ROM Dynamic simulation
  • Goal: Build experimental and simulated datasets to study and predict brake squeal occurrence.
  • Experimental: Multimodal measurements combining temperature, surface topography, contact states, and dynamic responses.
  • Numerical: Reduced-order modeling workflow enabling fast parameter sweeps and scalable synthetic data generation.
  • Outcome: Dataset used to train predictive models and support physically interpretable analyses.
